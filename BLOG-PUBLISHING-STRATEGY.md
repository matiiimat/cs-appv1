## 🎯 Publishing Rhythm: What Works Best for SEO

### ❌ **DON'T Publish All at Once**

Publishing 31 articles in one month = Bad idea because:
- Google sees it as suspicious/unnatural
- No time for articles to build authority
- You can't iterate based on what works
- Overwhelming for your audience
- Missed opportunities to build momentum

### ✅ **BEST Publishing Schedule: 3 Articles Per Week**

**Why 3/week is the sweet spot:**
- ✅ Consistent for Google (shows active site)
- ✅ Sustainable pace for quality content
- ✅ Gives each article time to rank
- ✅ Allows you to see what works and adjust
- ✅ Natural-looking growth pattern

**Timeline:**
- **31 articles ÷ 3 per week = ~10 weeks (2.5 months)**
- Realistically: **3 months with breaks**

## ✅ Placeholders are TOTALLY FINE for SEO!

### **Yes, you can have placeholder posts and add content later:**

**Google doesn't penalize for:**
- ❌ Empty pages that 404 (bad)
- ✅ Placeholder posts with frontmatter (fine)
- ✅ Gradual content addition (natural)

**Your placeholders have:**
- ✅ Proper titles and meta descriptions
- ✅ Category and keywords defined
- ✅ URL structure ready
- ✅ Sitemap entries

**When you add content:**
- Google re-crawls within days
- Rankings improve as content added
- No penalty for "thin content" initially
- Natural growth pattern

**Pro tip:** Add a simple intro paragraph to each placeholder:
```markdown
Coming soon: [Topic description]. Subscribe to our newsletter to be notified when this guide is published.
```

This prevents true "empty" pages while you write.

## 📝 Writing Brief for Your Content Writers

### **Article Requirements:**

**Length:**
- Tier 1: 2,000-2,500 words
- Tier 2: 1,500-2,000 words
- Original: 1,200-1,800 words

**Structure:**
- H1 title (in frontmatter)
- Introduction (150-200 words)
- H2 sections (5-8 major sections)
- H3 subsections as needed
- Conclusion with CTA
- Internal links (3-5 per post)

**Include:**
- Real examples
- Actionable templates/checklists
- Screenshots/images (1 per 500 words)
- Bullet points for scannability
- CTA to try Aidly (pricing page link)

**Tone:**
- Professional but conversational
- Second person ("you")
- Active voice
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- No fluff or filler

**SEO Requirements:**
- Primary keyword in title
- Primary keyword in first paragraph
- Primary keyword in at least one H2
- Keywords naturally throughout
- Alt text for all images
